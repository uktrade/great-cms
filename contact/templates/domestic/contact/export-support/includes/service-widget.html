VAT on services exports

All UK VAT registered businesses must charge VAT on the goods and services they sell unless they are exempt. Depending on place of supply rules, you may be able to charge zero rate on the services you export.

Show Hide

If you’re in the UK and the place of supply of your service is in the UK, you charge and account for VAT according to UK VAT rules.

If you’re in the UK and the place of supply of your service is in an EU country, you do not pay UK VAT. If the place of supply of your service is not in the EU, you do not have to charge EU VAT but you should include the sale on your VAT Return.

Understand withholding taxes when exporting services

Withholding tax is sometimes levied on income from service exports by overseas governments. If a withholding tax policy is in place, your overseas client will retain a percentage of the money owed and pay it directly to the local government.
